Brihadeeswarar Temple

In 1010 AD, it is one of the largest Hindu temples and an example of Tamil architecture. It is also called Dakshina Meru. Built by Chola emperor Rajaraja In between 1003 and 1010 CE, the temple is a part of the UNESCO. United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization.

World Heritage Site known as the "Great Living Chola Temples", along with the Chola-era Gangaikonda Cholapuram temple and Airavatesvara temple, which are about 70 kilometres (43 mi) and 40 kilometres (25 mi) to its northeast respectively.
